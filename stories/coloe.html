Coloe

The Coloured Bus Shelter

Coloe wants to improve a square called Piazza Vittoria in Como, an Italian city near Milan. After research, I discovered that this place has an important bus stop which doesn’t have proper street furniture for awaiting the bus and it is between historical and multiethnic areas of the city. My goal was to create a modular bus shelter that in the same time can represent Piazza Vittoria's soul as a meeting point of historical and multiethnic areas.

How can one represent historical and multiethnic areas in one object? I found the solution using coloured glass composition. The coloured glass composition can represent the historical area evoking the antique art of stained glass. After to represent the multiethnic area I decided to use colours of the Olympic symbol to represent five continents.

“My vision is to process images of any kind, architecture or design, or pictorial or sculptural interacting with people in a poetic way. If my object finds its friend it has played its role.” — A. Mendini

Coloe is a modular steel cantilever structure that can have different forms. The glass is inserted in a metallic frame with a Strip LED for the night. Also they have been designed a bench and a side coordinated. For the colours I used Vanceva by Serflex, a PVB interlayer to be inserted inside the double-glazed glass. These can be combined, inserting more than one on top of each other, to create a huge range of colours.
