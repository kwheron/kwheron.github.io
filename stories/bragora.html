Bragora

Tha Magic Square

University project in collaboration with Biagio Pandolfi, Andrea Pavan and Lorenzo Tomassetti. The goal was to design an identity and a promotional campaign for a campo (square in Venetian) of Venice. After research we discovered that Campo della Bragora stands out for having many legends that make this place mysterious and magical, from that comes out the design identity.

We designed San Marco stencil. San Marco was designed in 1990 by Karlgeorg Hoefer, inspired by the rotunda style of the fifteenth-century Italian gothic writing and gothic types designed by Nicolas Jenson as well as the roundness of the arches of the famous Basilica in Venice. The illustrations have been designed in woodcut style. They are five, one for each legend category: love, adventure, relics, spectra and death.

On the front of the map are reported the five thematic itineraries. Each route consists of five stages that will lead the reader to discover some of the many mysterious places of the lagoon through its legends. On the back, there is a short story of each of these legends divided into categories.

The installation in the field consists of five metal panels in which are carved the descriptions of the Bragora legends, written in San Marco stencil. During the day this pentagonal structure evokes the magical flow of the story and persons can enter in it to be immersed in the legends of the campo, but in the absence of light the installation expresses its charm, thanks to a structure of internal lighting the letters on the perforated panel are projected over the entire field, literally making the field an active part of the narration.
